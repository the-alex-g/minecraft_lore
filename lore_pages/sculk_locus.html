Sculk Locus

Obtaining

A sculk locus can be crafted from a sculk relic, three reinforced deepslate, and five echoing sculk.

Sculk locuses drop themselves when broken with any tool, but the fastest tool to mine them is a hoe.

Usage

A sculk locus grants passive status effects similar to beacons or conduits when placed on an appropriate base. A sculk locus is activated when in the middle of a 5x5 base of sculk and deepslate blocks. The blocks around the base must be either an ignored block or a slab block. The layer on top of the base, which includes the locus, must also be ignored blocks or slabs. The next layer, directly above the locus, must contain only ignored blocks. The whole base must contain at least 11 sculk-related blocks and 11 slabs. The locus itself must have a sky light level of 0 and a block light level of 10 or less.

The list of blocks ignored for the sculk locus base is as follows:

  • air
  • sculk mushrooms
  • shrillstalks
  • sculk mold
  • sculk fungus
  • echoing growths
  • sculk moss
  • sculk veins
  • soul torches
  • soul lanterns
  • sculk waypoints

The blocks counted as slabs for the locus base are any variant of deepslate slab, sculk sensors, and sculk shriekers.

While on an valid base, the sculk locus grants Haste II and a sneaking speed bonus for 12 seconds to any players whose eyes are in a block with sky light level 6 or less and a block light level of 10 or less and who are within a 21 block radius.
